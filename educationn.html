Questions for All Adopters:

  • 1 Do you have any other pets and how will they react to a new pet?
  • 2 Is your current residence suited to the pet you're considering?
  • 3 How will your social life or work obligations affect your ability to care for a pet?
  • 4 Do you have a plan for your new pet during vacations and/or work travel?
  • 5 How do the people you live with feel about having a pet in the house?

Size Considerations:

  • 1 What size pet can your home accommodate?
  • 2 Will you have enough room if your dog grows to be bigger than expected?
  • 3 What size pet would suit the other people who live in or visit your home regularly?
  • 4 Do you have another pet to consider when choosing the size of your next pet?
  • 5 How big a pet can you travel comfortably with?

Pet Costs:

  • 1 More likely than not, the adopting agency will charge a fee to help defray the cost of taking in unwanted or lost animals.
  • 2 You may need to pay for your adopted pet to be spayed or neutered before bringing him or her home.
  • 3 Some expenses are mandatory for all pets, including:
  • *Food
  • *Routine veterinary care
  • *Licensing according to local regulations
  • *Collars, leashes and identification tags
  • *Kitty litter and box
  • *Basic grooming equipment and supplies.

    • Other Considerations:

    • 1 What do you expect your pet to contribute to your life?
    • 2 If you are thinking of adopting a young animal, do you have the time and patience to work with the pet through its adolescence?
    • 3 Have you considered your lifestyle carefully and determined whether a younger or older animal would be a better match for you?
    • 4 Can you train and handle a pet with behavior issues or are you looking for an easy-going friend?
    • 5 Do you need a pet who will be reliable with children or one you can take with you when you travel?

Time Considerations:

  • 1 Pets need to be fed two to three times a day, more often in the case of puppies, and need a constant supply of fresh water.
  • 2 A responsible pet parent should spend at least one hour per day giving direct attention to his or her pet. This may include training, exercising, grooming, and playing or, with cats, may just be lap time on the couch. Dogs will need to be taken out to potty several times a day.
  • 3 A pet with an abundance of energy needs more time to exercise and interactive toys to keep them entertained
  • 3 Pets with long coats need 20 minutes a day of grooming to prevent matting.
